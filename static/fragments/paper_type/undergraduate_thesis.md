# Undergraduate Thesis Mode

## Target parameters
- Reference count: 30–50 papers (or user's specified count, whichever applies)
- Word count: 6000–12000 words for main body (or user's specified count)
- Main sections: 5–8

## Quality targets
- More thorough coverage of the topic landscape
- Explicit treatment of controversies, open questions, and evidence gaps
- Evidence strength noted where relevant (strong consensus vs. limited evidence)
- Boundary conditions acknowledged in the synthesis
- Background/fundamentals section to set the conceptual foundation

## Standard outline shape
1. Introduction
2. Background and Fundamentals
3. [Theme section 1]
4. [Theme section 2]
5. [Theme section 3–4 as needed]
6. Comparative Analysis (often benefits from a method comparison table)
7. Challenges and Future Directions
8. Conclusion

## Depth calibration for synthesis notes
Use all fields: `core_claims`, `method_groups`, `consensus_points`, `controversies`, `evidence_gaps`.
Synthesis notes for each section should be richer and reference more papers.

## Diagram guidance
- Generate 2–3 diagrams across the review
- Include at minimum: a conceptual overview diagram in the background section
- Add flow/architecture diagrams where the content involves processes or system structures

## Table guidance
- Generate comparison tables where 3+ methods or approaches are discussed
- Up to 2 tables is appropriate for a full thesis review

## Tone
Formal academic, comprehensive. Closer to a short journal review in rigor of expression.
