# English Writing Mode (en)

## Language register
- Formal academic English; avoid colloquialisms and hedging chains ("it can be seen that", "it is worth noting that")
- Active voice preferred for findings and comparisons; passive acceptable for methods
- Each paragraph opens with a clear topic sentence that makes a claim, not a meta-statement ("In this section, we review...")

## Terminology consistency
- Define abbreviations on first use: "convolutional neural network (CNN)"
- Maintain a terminology ledger across the review — one canonical form per concept
- Do not substitute synonyms to vary prose; consistency matters more than stylistic variation

## Citation format
- IEEE numeric style by default (ask user if APA is preferred for their institution)
- In-text: `[1]`, `[2,3]`, `[4]–[6]`
- Reference list: numbered in order of first citation
- Journal article format: `[n] A. B. Author, "Title of article," *Journal Name*, vol. X, no. Y, pp. Z–W, Year.`

## Paragraph structure
- 100–250 words per paragraph
- Structure: topic sentence → elaboration/comparison → evidence citations → limitation or transition
- Avoid sequential summaries: "Paper A found X. Paper B found Y." — synthesize instead
- Acknowledge disagreements explicitly: "While [3] reports X, subsequent work by [5] challenges this on the grounds that..."

## Figures and tables
- Figure caption below: `Figure X. [Caption text.]`
- Table caption above: `Table X. [Caption text.]`
- In-text reference: "as shown in Figure X" / "see Table X"
